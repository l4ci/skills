# force-field-analysis

Force field analysis is Kurt Lewin's way of looking at a proposed change, from his 1943 field theory. Any situation that holds steady is in equilibrium: driving forces push toward the change, restraining forces resist it and keep the status quo, and the two are in balance. The status quo is that balance point. You move toward what you want by shifting the balance, either pushing harder on the drivers or easing off the restrainers.

This skill runs the analysis in parallel. Finder subagents hunt the driving and restraining forces across different lenses, people, organizational, technical, and external, and score each one by strength with a rationale. A synthesis pass then builds the scored field, reads whether the change is feasible as things stand, and finds the highest-leverage moves to shift the balance.

Lewin's point, and the one the synthesis leans on, is that weakening a restrainer usually beats adding a driver. Pushing harder raises the tension and the restraining side tends to push back just as hard, so what you get is fragile and snaps back. Take a barrier away and the situation drifts toward the change and stays there. The skill also guards against the two ways this analysis goes wrong: a stacked list that is mostly drivers because nobody hunted the restrainers properly, and forces left unscored so the field can't actually be read. For leading a full change through to completion there is [kotter-change](../kotter-change/); for the individual adoption side there is [adkar](../adkar/).

## Starting

**You provide:** the change, decision, or goal being assessed, stated as a precise desired state; context on who's involved and the constraints is optional. Something like "do a force field analysis on moving support to a four-day week".
