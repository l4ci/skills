# Negotiation prep: Meridian Health senior engineer offer

> _Illustrative example output for the `negotiation-prep` skill. Meridian Health and all parties below are fictional; every figure, name, and claim below is invented to demonstrate the report's shape, not researched. Do not cite these numbers._

- **Negotiation:** Base-salary increase on a senior backend engineer offer from Meridian Health (a ~400-person health-tech firm).
- **Parties:** You (candidate, currently employed elsewhere at $158k) vs. Meridian Health, represented by the **VP of Engineering**, who holds sign-off on the offer. HR/recruiting drafts and relays; the VP decides the number.
- **Goal:** Land at **$185k base**; would accept **$178k** with extra equity. Respond by **Friday**.
- **Deadline:** Friday (≈3 days out).
- **Numbers on the table:** Meridian offer **$165k base**. Competing verbal offer elsewhere **$180k**. Metro market range for the role **$175k–$195k**.
- **Framework:** Fisher & Ury principled negotiation, anchored on BATNA and ZOPA. Preparation reduces but does not remove uncertainty: the other side's interests and walk-away are estimated, not known, and the plan will not survive first contact unchanged.

---

## 1. Verdict

**A deal is reachable, and the offer is very likely to move.** A zone of possible agreement (ZOPA) exists and is wide: your walk-away sits at **$180k** (your competing offer), and Meridian's reservation point is estimated at **~$188k–$192k**: the top of where a 400-person firm will go for a senior backend hire it has already chosen and wants to close this week. The overlap, roughly **$180k–$190k**, is where any agreement that beats both sides' alternatives lives.

- **Target (anchor toward):** **$190k**, justified by the market top and your competing offer.
- **Realistic settle:** **$184k–$187k** base, or **$178k–$180k** base plus an equity bump if they cite a band ceiling.
- **Walk-away:** below **$180k base-equivalent** total comp, at that point your competing offer is the better deal.

The $165k opening is below your competing offer and below the local market floor for the role; it is an anchor, not a ceiling. The lever is not pleading, it is the documented competing offer plus published market data, which lets you re-anchor on objective criteria rather than haggle from their $165k. The one thing that could collapse the deal is a hard internal salary band; that is the assumption most worth testing first (§6).

## 2. Interests (positions vs. interests beneath them)

A position is the number each side states. The interest is *why*. Trade on the why.

### Your interests, ranked

| Rank | Interest | Position it drives | Type | Fact / assumption |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Economic floor + recognition**: be paid at least at market and visibly above your current $158k; the raise validates the "senior" title | "I want $185k" | Must-have | Fact (your stated goal and current salary) |
| 2 | **Not leaving money on the table**: a competing offer at $180k means accepting $165k feels like a $15k+ self-inflicted loss | Push the base up | Must-have | Fact |
| 3 | **Total-comp upside**: equity/bonus can substitute for some base; you explicitly named equity as a lever | Accept $178k base *with* extra equity | Tradeable | Fact (stated) |
| 4 | **Role and growth**: this is presumably a better role/mission than your current job, or you would not be negotiating it | Get to yes, not just get a number | Nice-to-have (high) | Assumption: you have not said why Meridian over staying |
| 5 | **Relationship with the VP**: you will report to the person you are negotiating with; do not win the salary and lose the manager | Negotiate on principle, not pressure | Must-have (process) | Assumption (standard reporting structure) |
| 6 | **Speed / certainty**: resolve by Friday without a drawn-out back-and-forth | Respond this week | Nice-to-have | Fact (deadline stated) |

**Must-haves:** market-rate pay that clears your competing offer; preserving the working relationship with your future boss.
**Tradeable:** base-vs-equity mix, start date, signing bonus, title nuance, the exact Friday timing.
**Nice-to-haves:** the role itself, a fast clean close.

### Meridian's likely interests

| Their likely interest | Position it drives | Type | Fact / assumption |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Close this hire**: senior backend roles are slow and costly to fill; they already chose you | Make an offer that sticks | Strong | Assumption (they extended an offer = real intent) |
| **Internal pay equity**: paying you well above existing seniors creates compression and morale risk | Anchor low at $165k; resist a big jump | Strong | Assumption (universal at ~400-person firms) |
| **Budget / band discipline**: the VP owns a comp budget and a defined band per level | Hold a ceiling | Strong | Assumption: band ceiling unknown |
| **Don't overpay vs. perceived alternatives**: if they think you have no other offer, $165k looks generous vs. your $158k | Test whether you'll just say yes | Medium | Assumption |
| **VP's own credibility**: the VP wants to land a strong hire without looking like a pushover to their own leadership | Want a justifiable, not arbitrary, final number | Medium | Assumption |
| **Speed**: an open senior req is a drag on the team; they also want this done | Decide quickly | Medium | Fact-adjacent (they set/accepted a Friday timeline) |

**The key complementarity (the orange):** you value base highly (it sets your floor and follows you); Meridian values *protecting the published band and equity equity* highly (base raises set precedent for every future hire; equity and signing bonuses do not). That gap is exactly where a mutual-gain trade lives: a one-time signing bonus or extra equity costs them less precedent than the same dollars in base, and may cost you little if total comp clears $180k.

## 3. Alternatives and the ZOPA

### Your BATNA

**Take the competing verbal offer at $180k base.** This is a strong, concrete alternative, it is the single fact that converts this from pleading into negotiating.

- **Strength:** High *if it is real and firm*. A **verbal** offer is weaker than a written one: it can be withdrawn, the number can soften, and "verbal" gives Meridian room to discount it.
- **Reservation point it implies:** **$180k base-equivalent.** Below the total-comp value of the competing offer, walking to that offer is the better outcome. (If Meridian's role/equity/mission is materially better, your true walk-away could sit a little under $180k, but do not concede that publicly.)
- **Fallback below BATNA:** stay in your current $158k role. This is a weak third option and should never be revealed; it would invite a low anchor.

**How to strengthen your BATNA before Friday:**
1. **Get the competing offer in writing** (offer letter or email confirming base, equity, deadline). A written $180k is dramatically more credible at the table than "a verbal."
2. **Confirm its own deadline** so you are not bluffing about timing; align or sequence the two.
3. Keep the competing employer warm, a live, responsive alternative is leverage; a stale one is not.

### Meridian's likely BATNA

**Re-open the search or extend to their #2 candidate.** Estimated **weak-to-moderate**, which favors you.

- Senior backend searches at a 400-person health-tech firm typically run 8–14 weeks with loaded sourcing/interview cost; a #2 candidate is by definition the worse hire and may have moved on.
- They have invested interview cycles in you and made an offer, sunk cost plus revealed preference both say they want *you*, not a restart.
- **Their reservation point (estimated):** **~$188k–$192k.** Above that, re-opening the search likely looks cheaper than overpaying and breaking the band. The closer your ask sits to the market top ($195k), the more their band/equity discipline pushes back.

### The ZOPA

| | Walk-away (reservation point) | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **You** | **$180k** base-equivalent | Competing offer (BATNA) |
| **Meridian** | **~$188k–$192k** | Cost of re-searching vs. band/equity discipline (estimated) |

**ZOPA ≈ $180k – $190k base-equivalent.** A real, comfortably wide zone. Any number in it beats both sides' alternatives, so a deal *should* close. Two caveats:

- The lower bound is only as firm as your competing offer is real: verbal-not-written narrows it.
- The upper bound is an **assumption** built on a guessed band ceiling. If Meridian's senior band tops out at, say, $180k base, the ZOPA in *base* collapses to a point, and the deal has to migrate to **total comp** (base + signing bonus + equity) to stay open. That single uncertainty is why §6 leads with it.

Do not mistake your **$185k goal** for the ZOPA; the goal sits inside it, which is good news, but the zone is set by both reservation points, not by your aspiration.

## 4. The plan

### Target and walk-away

- **Target / opening anchor:** **$190k base**: at the top of the cited market range, justified, not arbitrary.
- **Aim to settle:** **$184k–$187k** base; or **$178k–$180k** base + a signing bonus / extra equity if they invoke a band ceiling.
- **Walk-away:** below **$180k** base-equivalent total comp. Hold it; it is your BATNA, not a comfort number.

### Move sequence

**1. Re-anchor on criteria, not on their $165k.** Open warm and collaborative, then anchor:

> "I'm genuinely excited about the role and the team, I want to make this work. The piece I need to align is base. Market for this role in [metro] runs $175k–$195k, and I'm holding a competing offer at $180k. Given that, I was expecting something closer to **$190k**. Can we get there?"

This invokes two **objective criteria** at once, published market range and a comparable competing offer, and re-anchors away from $165k toward the market top. Never counter off their $165k; that concedes the frame.

**2. Concede in shrinking, slowing steps, each traded for something.** Plan the give-backs in advance so they read as approaching your limit:

| Step | You move to | Trade you ask for |
|---|---|---|
| Opening | $190k |: (anchor) |
| 1st concession | $187k | Faster start / they cover the gap with signing bonus |
| 2nd (smaller) | $185k | Equity bump or accelerated comp review at 6 months |
| Floor | $182k–$180k base **+ equity/bonus** | Only if they cite a hard band; migrate value to total comp |

Make each concession smaller and slower than the last. Trade base you value highly for things *they* part with cheaply (signing bonus, equity, title, start date): the orange split: take the half each side values most.

**3. Objective criteria to invoke per issue:**

| Contested issue | Standard to invoke |
|---|---|
| Base number | Metro market range $175k–$195k; the $180k competing offer; comp survey/levels data for "senior backend, [metro]" |
| "Our band tops at X" | Ask for the band and where the role sits in it; propose total-comp parity instead of base parity |
| Equity value | Strike price, last-round valuation, vesting: value it honestly, don't accept "it's worth a lot" |
| Timing pressure | Your competing offer's actual deadline: a real date, used as fact, not as a bluff |

### Prepared responses to their likely tactics

| Likely tactic | Why they'd use it | Your principled response |
|---|---|---|
| **Low anchor held** ("$165k is our offer") | Sees if you'll accept; protects the band | Don't haggle up from it. Return to criteria: market range + competing offer. "$165k is below market and below a written offer I'm holding: help me understand the gap." |
| **"That's outside the band / our hands are tied"** | Band discipline, or a soft cap | Test it: ask the band and the level placement. If real, **migrate to total comp**: signing bonus + equity to bridge to $180k+. Bands constrain base, rarely one-time cash. |
| **Discount your BATNA** ("verbal offers fall through") | Weakens your leverage | Have it **in writing**. Offer to show the letter (redacted). A documented $180k cannot be waved away. |
| **False / compressed deadline** ("we need yes today") | Pressure to skip the negotiation | Name it calmly: "Friday works for me; I want to get the number right, not just fast." Use *your* real deadline as the fixed point. |
| **Appeal to relationship / mission** ("we're a team, money isn't everything") | Soften you off the number | Agree on the relationship, separate it from the problem: "I'm fully in on the mission: which is exactly why I want the comp set fairly so it's a non-issue once I start." |
| **"Take it or leave it"** | Test resolve | Don't react to the frame. Restate criteria + BATNA: "At market and with a written $180k offer, I can't go below $180k total. If $180k+ isn't possible, I understand: but I'd hate to lose this over a gap we can bridge with equity." |

Throughout: separate the people from the problem (you're negotiating *with* your future boss), insist on objective criteria, and let your BATNA: not emotion, set the floor.

## 5. Assumptions to test

Ranked by how much being wrong would change the plan.

1. **Is the competing $180k offer real, firm, and (ideally) in writing?**
   *If wrong:* your BATNA and the entire ZOPA floor evaporate; you'd be negotiating from $158k, and $165k starts to look defensible.
   *Test before Friday:* get it in writing or at least a confirming email; know its deadline. This is the single highest-leverage action this week.

2. **Does Meridian's senior band cap base below ~$185k?**
   *If wrong (band caps low):* the base ZOPA collapses; the deal must move to total comp (signing bonus + equity). You'd shift your ask, not your walk-away.
   *Test at the table:* ask directly, "What's the band for this level, and where does the offer sit in it?" Their answer reframes the whole negotiation toward equity.

3. **How badly do they need to close *now* vs. re-search?**
   *If wrong (they're relaxed about re-opening):* their reservation point drops toward the band ceiling and your upside shrinks.
   *Test:* read urgency signals, how fast they moved you through interviews, whether they've stressed the team's need, how quickly recruiting responds.

4. **Is your real reason for choosing Meridian over staying / the other offer strong (interest #4)?**
   *If wrong (you're indifferent):* you have less to lose by walking, which *strengthens* your hand, but also means don't over-concede to land a role you don't clearly prefer.
   *Test:* be honest with yourself before Friday about why Meridian, so the relationship interest doesn't quietly pull you below your walk-away.

---

## 6. Caveats

- Meridian's interests, BATNA, and especially its salary band are **estimated, not known**. The ~$188k–$192k reservation point and the band-ceiling risk are assumptions; the plan is only as good as those estimates, which is why §5 ranks them for testing.
- A mapped ZOPA describes **room for a deal, not a guarantee of one.** An internal band, a budget freeze, or a VP who simply won't break precedent can close the zone even when the math says it's open.
- **People don't always negotiate rationally.** Ego, an awkward exchange, or a misread tone can derail a deal that the numbers say should close, hence separating the people from the problem, especially with someone who will be your manager.
- Your BATNA is only as strong as the competing offer is **real and documented.** A verbal offer that softens or vanishes pulls the floor out from under everything here.
- Preparation **informs the room but does not survive contact unchanged.** Hold the framework, interests, BATNA, ZOPA, criteria, and adapt the specific numbers to what you actually learn at the table.
- All figures in this report are illustrative for documentation purposes and were not researched.
