## Profile interactions

Single-trait readouts miss how these combine. Four combinations do most of the explanatory work here.

1. **Very high Honesty-Humility × average Greed-Avoidance, the principled comfort-lover.** Almost all of her H is at the ceiling: she won't cheat, flatter, or trade on status. But she openly loves luxury and good restaurants. This is not hypocrisy; it's the difference between *being driven by acquisition* (she isn't) and *enjoying nice things* (she does). The watch-point is small and honest: a high-integrity person with a real taste for comfort can over-spend without ever bending a rule. The integrity is rock-solid; the budget is the soft edge.

2. **Very high Sentimentality × low Dependence × very high Patience, the absorbent stoic.** She feels everyone's emotions intensely (Sentimentality), almost never asks for support (low Dependence), and rarely shows irritation (high Patience). That combination means distress flows *in* and very little flows *out*. She'll be the steady, empathetic one people lean on, and the one whose own accumulated weight nobody notices, because she neither complains nor leans back. A textbook quiet-burnout shape.

3. **Low eXtraversion × very high Conscientiousness × very high Honesty-Humility, the invisible reliable.** She does excellent, careful, honest work (C + H) but won't draw attention to it, won't self-advocate, and finds public speaking frightening (very low Expressiveness and Social Boldness). High contributors who refuse the spotlight are routinely under-credited. Her integrity means she won't even claim the credit she's owed. Worth being deliberate about visibility.

4. **High Agreeableness (temper) × low Flexibility × very high Perfectionism, patient with people, rigid about process.** She won't lose her temper at *you*, but she has a fixed idea of how things should be arranged and done, and others' mess or sloppiness genuinely grates (low Flexibility + ceiling Perfectionism). The friction won't show as anger; it'll show as quiet tension, redoing others' work, or being thrown when someone reorders her system. The even temper can mask how much the disorder costs her.

## Caveats and how to read this

- **Self-report, one day.** This captures how you saw yourself today, not a fixed truth. Mood and recent events shift answers.
- **No norms.** The bands are heuristic descriptions of *your own* answer pattern, not a percentile ranking against the general population. This preliminary IPIP-HEXACO set ships without official norms.
- **Facets are indicative, not precise.** Each facet rests on only 10 items, and the trimmed final HEXACO scales are not in the public domain, so facet readings (especially the splits this report leans on) point a direction rather than fix a coordinate.
- **Continua, not types.** "Very high" and "low" are positions on a scale, not boxes. Borderline factors here (eXtraversion landed right at the low/average line) could read either way on a different day.
- **Honesty-Humility is the point.** It is what HEXACO adds over the Big Five and the strongest predictor in the model of exploitation, entitlement, and integrity, which is why this report gives it full weight rather than a footnote.
- **Not clinical.** This is a non-clinical self-report questionnaire, not a diagnostic instrument. For any mental-health concern, consult a professional.
