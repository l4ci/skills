# IPIP Big-Five Factor Markers (50 items)

Public-domain instrument from the International Personality Item Pool (Goldberg, 1992). Source: [ipip.ori.org](https://ipip.ori.org/New_IPIP-50-item-scale.htm). Each factor is measured by 10 items.

## Mapping to OCEAN

The IPIP-50 measures **Emotional Stability**. OCEAN's **Neuroticism** is its inverse, so the keying below is already flipped for Neuroticism: a high Neuroticism score means low emotional stability. The other four factors map directly.

- **O** = Openness (IPIP "Intellect/Imagination")
- **C** = Conscientiousness
- **E** = Extraversion
- **A** = Agreeableness
- **N** = Neuroticism (inverse of IPIP "Emotional Stability")

## Administration

Present every item as a self-statement the taker rates on a 5-point Likert scale:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Disagree strongly | Disagree a little | Neither agree nor disagree | Agree a little | Agree strongly |

Keep the original item order (it interleaves the five factors, which reduces response-set bias). Translate the items faithfully into the taker's language if it is not English. Each item is the sentence stem "I ..." (for example, "I am the life of the party").

## Scoring

1. Each answer is a number from 1 to 5.
2. For a `+` keyed item, the contribution is the raw answer. For a `-` keyed item, the contribution is `6 - answer`.
3. Sum the 10 contributions for each factor. The factor total ranges from 10 to 50.
4. The per-item average is `total / 10`, ranging from 1.0 to 5.0. Use the average for bands and the total for the record.

## Bands

These bands are heuristic interpretive aids, not population percentiles. The IPIP-50 ships without official norms, so the report must say the bands describe the taker's relative answer pattern, not a ranking against the general population.

| Per-item average | Band |
|---|---|
| 1.0 to 2.0 | very low |
| 2.1 to 2.6 | low |
| 2.7 to 3.4 | average |
| 3.5 to 4.0 | high |
| 4.1 to 5.0 | very high |
