# psychological-safety

Diagnoses a team's psychological safety and prescribes how to raise it. The construct comes from Amy Edmondson (*The Fearless Organization*, 2019, building on her 1999 research): psychological safety is the shared belief that a team is safe for interpersonal risk-taking, speaking up, admitting mistakes, asking for help, challenging, without fear of being punished or humiliated. It is a property of the team climate, not of any individual, and it is one of the strongest predictors of team learning and, through learning, of performance.

This skill runs the diagnosis in parallel. Five assessor subagents each take one dimension of the climate, voice and speaking up, response to mistakes, help-seeking, inclusion of difference, and risk-taking and challenge, and score it against Edmondson's behavioral markers with real evidence, judging the team rather than the people in it. A synthesis pass then places the team on the safety-by-accountability 2x2, names the weakest dimensions and the behaviors driving them, and prescribes interventions against Edmondson's three leader practices: frame the work as a learning problem, acknowledge your own fallibility, and model curiosity by asking questions. The output is a saved markdown report with the climate read dimension by dimension, the team plotted on the 2x2, and a prescription that points at the learning zone.

Three misreadings are common, and the skill holds the line against each. People hear "psychological safety" and think niceness, so they chase a warm, agreeable, conflict-free team, which is often the least safe team there is, because the warmth is what stops anyone raising the hard thing. People also treat it as an individual trait rather than a climate, and, worst of all, they lower standards in the name of safety and slide into the comfort zone. The skill holds the line that the target is high safety paired with high accountability, the learning zone, that the climate is read from observable behavior and not from a vibe, and that no prescription is allowed to trade away the standard.
