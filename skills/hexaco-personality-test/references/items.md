# IPIP-HEXACO scales (240 items)

Public-domain instrument from the International Personality Item Pool. The items are the preliminary IPIP scales built to measure the constructs in Lee and Ashton's HEXACO model (Ashton, Lee, & Goldberg, 2007, *The IPIP–HEXACO scales*, Personality and Individual Differences, 42, 1515–1526). Source: [ipip.ori.org/newHEXACO_PI_key.htm](https://ipip.ori.org/newHEXACO_PI_key.htm). Six factors, four facets each, ten items per facet.

The IPIP is public domain: "Because the IPIP has been placed in the public domain, permission has already been automatically granted for any person to use IPIP items, scales, and inventories for any purpose, commercial or non-commercial" ([ipip.ori.org/newPermission.htm](https://ipip.ori.org/newPermission.htm)).

## The six factors

HEXACO is the Big Five plus a sixth factor, and its Emotionality and Agreeableness are rotated relative to Big Five Neuroticism and Agreeableness.

- **H** = Honesty-Humility (Sincerity, Fairness, Greed-Avoidance, Modesty), the factor OCEAN/Big Five lacks. High H means sincere, fair, modest, unmotivated by status and luxury; low H means manipulative, entitled, status-driven.
- **E** = Emotionality (Fearfulness, Anxiety, Dependence, Sentimentality), vulnerability and attachment, not the hostility-tinged Big Five Neuroticism.
- **X** = eXtraversion (Expressiveness, Social Boldness, Sociability, Liveliness).
- **A** = Agreeableness (Forgiveness, Gentleness, Flexibility, Patience), anchored on tolerance and even temper. (Low A overlaps the anger pole that Big Five puts under Neuroticism.)
- **C** = Conscientiousness (Organization, Diligence, Perfectionism, Prudence).
- **O** = Openness to Experience (Aesthetic Appreciation, Inquisitiveness, Creativity, Unconventionality).

## Administration

This is the 240-item set, so it is long (roughly 30–40 minutes). Present every item as a self-statement completing the stem **"I …"** that the taker rates on a 5-point accuracy scale:

| 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Very Inaccurate | Moderately Inaccurate | Neither Inaccurate nor Accurate | Moderately Accurate | Very Accurate |

The instruction stem (IPIP standard) is: "Describe yourself as you generally are now, not as you wish to be in the future. Describe yourself as you honestly see yourself." Each item completes "I …" (for example, item 81 reads "I talk a lot."). When showing an item, prepend "I" and lowercase its first word for natural reading; keep the scoring keyed to the item number regardless of how it is displayed.

Unlike the IPIP-50, this set is listed grouped by factor and facet rather than interleaved. To reduce response-set bias you may present the items in a shuffled order, but you must track each item's number so scoring stays correct. Translate the items faithfully into the taker's language if it is not English.

## Items and keying

The key column says how the item counts toward its factor: `+` means a higher rating raises the score, `−` means it lowers it (reverse-keyed). Each item completes "I …".

## Items grouped by factor and facet

Each factor has 40 items (four facets of 10). Item-number ranges:

- **H (Honesty-Humility):** 1–40: Sincerity 1–10, Fairness 11–20, Greed-Avoidance 21–30, Modesty 31–40
- **E (Emotionality):** 41–80, Fearfulness 41–50, Anxiety 51–60, Dependence 61–70, Sentimentality 71–80
- **X (eXtraversion):** 81–120, Expressiveness 81–90, Social Boldness 91–100, Sociability 101–110, Liveliness 111–120
- **A (Agreeableness):** 121–160, Forgiveness 121–130, Gentleness 131–140, Flexibility 141–150, Patience 151–160
- **C (Conscientiousness):** 161–200, Organization 161–170, Diligence 171–180, Perfectionism 181–190, Prudence 191–200
- **O (Openness):** 201–240, Aesthetic Appreciation 201–210, Inquisitiveness 211–220, Creativity 221–230, Unconventionality 231–240

## Scoring

1. Each answer is a number from 1 to 5.
2. For a `+` keyed item, the contribution is the raw answer. For a `−` keyed item, the contribution is `6 - answer`.
3. **Facet score:** mean of the 10 contributions in the facet, ranging from 1.0 to 5.0.
4. **Factor score:** mean of the 40 contributions in the factor, ranging from 1.0 to 5.0. (IPIP's official method sums the items; the mean is used here so factors and facets are on the same comparable 1.0–5.0 scale.)
5. Assign each factor and facet a band from the bands table using the per-item mean.

## Bands

These bands are heuristic interpretive aids, not population percentiles. This preliminary IPIP-HEXACO set ships without official norms, so the report must say the bands describe the taker's relative answer pattern, not a ranking against the general population.

| Per-item mean | Band |
|---|---|
| 1.0 to 2.0 | very low |
| 2.1 to 2.6 | low |
| 2.7 to 3.4 | average |
| 3.5 to 4.0 | high |
| 4.1 to 5.0 | very high |
